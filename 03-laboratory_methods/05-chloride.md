# Chloride

> *Extracted from: Evaluation of Water Quality Index for River Mogral (2021)*
> *Source pages (PDF): 33–34*

## Apparatus

* Burette
* Pipette
* Erlenmeyer flask
* Measuring cylinder

## Reagents

* Chloride-free distilled water
* Standard silver nitrate solution (0.0141 N)
* Potassium chromate indicator
* Acid or alkali for pH adjustment

## Procedure

### Sample Titration

1. Rinse the burette with silver nitrate solution and fill it with the same solution.
2. Take 50 mL of the sample in a clean 250 mL conical flask and dilute it to 100 mL.
3. Adjust the sample pH to 7–8 using acid or alkali if required.
4. Add 1 mL of potassium chromate indicator to obtain a light yellow colour.
5. Titrate against the silver nitrate solution until the colour changes from yellow to brick red, indicating the end point.
6. Record the volume of silver nitrate used.

### Blank Titration

1. Take 50 mL of chloride-free distilled water in a clean conical flask.
2. Add 1 mL potassium chromate indicator.
3. Titrate with silver nitrate solution until the colour changes from yellow to brick red.
4. Record the volume of silver nitrate used for the blank.
