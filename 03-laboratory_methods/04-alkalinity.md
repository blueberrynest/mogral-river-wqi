# Alkalinity

> *Extracted from: Evaluation of Water Quality Index for River Mogral (2021)*
> *Source pages (PDF): 32–33*

## Apparatus

* Burette
* Pipette
* Erlenmeyer flask
* Measuring cylinder
* Wash bottle and beakers

## Reagents

* 0.02 N sulphuric acid
* Phenolphthalein indicator
* Methyl orange indicator

## Procedure

1. Rinse the burette with 0.02 N sulphuric acid and fill it with the same solution.
2. Fix the burette to the stand and adjust the initial reading to zero.
3. Measure exactly 50 mL of the water sample and transfer it into a 250 mL conical flask.
4. Add a few drops of phenolphthalein indicator. The solution turns pink if hydroxyl alkalinity is present.
5. Titrate with 0.02 N sulphuric acid until the pink colour disappears. Record the titre value to determine phenolphthalein alkalinity.
6. Add a few drops of methyl orange indicator to the same sample. The solution turns yellow.
7. Continue titration with sulphuric acid until the solution changes to orange-yellow (pH ≈ 4.5).
8. Record the total volume of acid used to determine total alkalinity.
