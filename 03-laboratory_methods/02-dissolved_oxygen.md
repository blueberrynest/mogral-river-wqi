# Dissolved Oxygen (DO)

> *Extracted from: Evaluation of Water Quality Index for River Mogral (2021)*
> *Source pages (PDF): 29–30*

## Apparatus

* Burette
* Pipette
* 300 mL glass stoppered BOD bottles
* Conical flask
* Measuring cylinder

## Reagents

* Manganous sulphate solution (MnSO₄·4H₂O)
* Alkali–iodide–azide reagent
* Concentrated sulphuric acid (36 N)
* Starch indicator
* Standard sodium thiosulphate solution (0.025 N)

## Procedure

1. Take two 300 mL glass stoppered BOD bottles and fill them with the water sample, avoiding any air bubbles.
2. Add 2 mL of manganous sulphate solution below the surface of the liquid using a pipette.
3. Add 2 mL of alkali–iodide–azide reagent in the same manner. A brownish-orange precipitate will form if oxygen is present.
4. Insert the stopper carefully to exclude air bubbles and mix by inverting the bottle at least 15 times.
5. Allow the precipitate to settle and shake again.
6. After about two minutes, remove the stopper and add 2 mL of concentrated sulphuric acid along the neck of the bottle.
7. Replace the stopper and mix by gentle inversion until the precipitate dissolves completely.
8. Rinse and fill the burette with sodium thiosulphate solution.
9. Measure 203 mL of the solution from the bottle into an Erlenmeyer flask.
10. Titrate with 0.025 N sodium thiosulphate solution until the solution becomes pale straw in colour.
11. Add 1–2 mL of starch indicator and continue titration until the blue colour disappears.
12. Record the volume of sodium thiosulphate used. This value directly gives the dissolved oxygen concentration in mg/L.
