# Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD)

> *Extracted from: Evaluation of Water Quality Index for River Mogral (2021)*
> *Source pages (PDF): 31–32*

## Apparatus

* BOD bottles (300 mL capacity)
* BOD incubator
* Burette
* Pipette
* Conical flask
* Measuring cylinder

## Reagents

* Manganous sulphate solution (MnSO₄·4H₂O)
* Alkali–iodide–azide reagent
* Concentrated sulphuric acid (36 N)
* Starch indicator
* Standard sodium thiosulphate solution (0.025 N)

## Procedure

1. Take two 300 mL BOD bottles and fill them with the water sample, avoiding air bubbles.
2. Add the required volume of sample to each bottle and fill the remaining portion with dilution water.
3. Place one bottle in a BOD incubator at 20°C for five days. The second bottle is analysed immediately to determine the initial dissolved oxygen (DO).
4. Add 2 mL of manganous sulphate solution to the bottle.
5. Add 2 mL of alkali–iodide–azide reagent. A brownish precipitate will form if oxygen is present.
6. Stopper the bottle carefully and mix by inverting it several times.
7. Allow the precipitate to settle and then shake again.
8. After two minutes, add 2 mL concentrated sulphuric acid and mix until the precipitate dissolves.
9. Titrate the sample with 0.025 N sodium thiosulphate solution using starch indicator as described in the DO determination procedure to measure the initial DO.
10. After incubation, analyse the second bottle for final dissolved oxygen using the same titration method.

The difference between the initial and final dissolved oxygen concentrations represents the biochemical oxygen demand of the sample.
