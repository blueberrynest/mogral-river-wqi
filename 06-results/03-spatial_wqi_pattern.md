# Spatial WQI Pattern

> Extracted from: *Evaluation of Water Quality Index for River Mogral (2021)*
> Source pages (PDF): 41, 48–50

## Water Quality Index Assessment

The Water Quality Index (WQI) for the Mogral River was calculated using the **Weighted Arithmetic Water Quality Index method**. The index integrates multiple physicochemical parameters into a single value representing the overall water quality condition.

The calculated WQI values for the sampling stations are presented in Table 5.6 of the original report.

## Spatial Variation of Water Quality

The WQI values observed across the sampling stations indicate spatial variation in water quality along the river.

During **January**, water quality conditions were generally classified as **Good** across most stations. However:

* **Site S6 and Site S22** were classified as **Poor**.
* **Sites S23 and S24** were classified as **Very Poor**.

During **February**, water quality conditions changed slightly:

* **Site S6 improved** and was categorized as **Good**.
* **Sites S14 and S18** were categorized as **Poor**.
* **Site S22** was classified as **Very Poor**.
* **Site S24** deteriorated further and was classified as **Unsuitable for drinking**.

## Downstream Water Quality Degradation

The deterioration in water quality at downstream stations is primarily associated with **high chloride concentrations caused by seawater intrusion**. Stations located closer to the river mouth exhibited significantly higher WQI values, indicating poorer water quality.

## Interpretation of WQI Trends

Overall, the WQI results suggest that:

* Upstream sections of the river generally maintain **acceptable water quality conditions**.
* Downstream regions experience **increasing salinity and water quality degradation**.
* Seawater intrusion and anthropogenic influences contribute to the observed spatial variation in water quality.
