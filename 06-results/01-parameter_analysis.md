# Parameter Analysis

> Extracted from: *Evaluation of Water Quality Index for River Mogral (2021)*
> Source pages (PDF): 38–40

## Overview

This study evaluates variations in physicochemical water quality parameters of the Mogral River during January and February 2021 across twenty-four sampling stations (S1–S24). The parameters analyzed include temperature, pH, dissolved oxygen (DO), biochemical oxygen demand (BOD), chlorides, and alkalinity.

## Temperature

Water temperature plays a critical role in aquatic environments because it influences biochemical reactions and metabolic processes of aquatic organisms. Increased water temperature accelerates chemical reactions, reduces the solubility of gases, and can intensify taste and odor problems.

In the present study, water temperature ranged from **28 °C to 30 °C**, indicating relatively stable thermal conditions across the sampling period.

## pH

The observed pH values ranged between **6.1 and 7.26**, which generally fall within the acceptable limits recommended by drinking water standards. Most sampling locations complied with the Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) guidelines.

Only a few stations exhibited slightly lower pH values, but overall variation remained minimal. This narrow variation in pH is attributed to limited annual fluctuations in dissolved carbon dioxide (CO₂) levels in the river system.

## Dissolved Oxygen (DO)

Dissolved oxygen concentrations ranged between **5.8 mg/L and 7.9 mg/L**, which are within acceptable limits for aquatic ecosystems. Adequate DO levels indicate relatively good aeration and support for aquatic life.

Among the stations, **Site S18 recorded the lowest dissolved oxygen concentration**, suggesting localized organic loading or reduced oxygen exchange in that region.

## Biochemical Oxygen Demand (BOD)

The BOD values observed in the study ranged between **0 mg/L and 2.1 mg/L**. Lower BOD levels indicate minimal organic pollution and reduced microbial oxygen consumption.

The highest BOD concentration was observed at **Site S18**, indicating relatively higher organic matter presence at that location.

## Alkalinity

Total alkalinity values ranged from **10 mg/L to 130 mg/L**, remaining within the permissible limits for drinking water according to BIS standards. The observed alkalinity levels suggest moderate buffering capacity of the river water.

## Chlorides

Chloride concentrations exhibited a **wide range from 18 mg/L to 18,899.47 mg/L**. Several sampling stations recorded chloride concentrations significantly higher than recommended limits.

Elevated chloride levels are commonly associated with **seawater intrusion**, particularly in coastal river systems where tidal influence can transport saline water upstream.

In January, many sampling stations showed high chloride concentrations. In February, chloride levels decreased slightly, although several stations still exceeded permissible limits.

High chloride concentrations can degrade water quality and reduce the suitability of water for drinking, agricultural, and industrial uses.
