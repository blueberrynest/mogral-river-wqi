# Chloride Intrusion

> Extracted from: *Evaluation of Water Quality Index for River Mogral (2021)*
> Source pages (PDF): 38–41

## Chloride Distribution in Mogral River

Chloride concentration is a critical indicator of salinity intrusion in coastal river systems. In the present study, chloride levels varied significantly across sampling stations, ranging from **18 mg/L to 18,899.47 mg/L**.

Several downstream stations recorded chloride concentrations that were far higher than the recommended drinking water limits.

## Evidence of Seawater Intrusion

The extremely high chloride values observed at certain sampling locations are attributed to **seawater intrusion from the Arabian Sea**. Tidal influence allows saline water to move upstream during high tide, increasing chloride concentrations in the lower reaches of the river.

This phenomenon is particularly pronounced in coastal rivers with low freshwater discharge during dry seasons.

## Seasonal Variation

The study observed noticeable seasonal variation in chloride levels between January and February.

* **January:** Higher chloride concentrations were recorded at many stations.
* **February:** Chloride levels decreased slightly at several locations, though some stations continued to exceed standard limits.

These variations are likely influenced by changes in tidal dynamics and freshwater inflow.

## Impact on Water Quality

Elevated chloride concentrations have significant implications for water quality. High salinity levels can:

* Reduce suitability of water for drinking
* Affect agricultural irrigation
* Increase corrosion in industrial systems
* Alter aquatic ecosystems

Therefore, chloride intrusion represents a key factor influencing water quality deterioration in the downstream sections of the Mogral River.
