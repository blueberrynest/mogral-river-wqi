# Climate

> *Extracted from: Evaluation of Water Quality Index for River Mogral (2021)*
> *Source pages (PDF): 14, 15, 16*

The Mogral River basin experiences a humid tropical climate typical of the coastal regions of Kerala. The district receives an average annual rainfall of approximately 3500 mm. The majority of precipitation occurs during the southwest monsoon season from June to September, contributing nearly 85.3% of the total annual rainfall. The northeast monsoon contributes about 8.9%, while the remaining 5.8% is received as pre-monsoon showers between January and May. Out of approximately 106 rainy days in a year, about 87 occur during the southwest monsoon period.

Temperatures are generally higher during the months of March to May and lower during December and January. The average mean monthly maximum temperature ranges from 29.2 °C to 33.4 °C, while the minimum temperature ranges from 19.7 °C to 25 °C.

Relative humidity is typically high in the region. During morning hours it ranges from about 87.1% to 98.7%, while evening humidity varies from approximately 54.4% to 86.5%.

Evaporation rates are higher during the summer months of March to May. In general, evaporation ranges between 2.2 and 6.3 mm per day, while during the southwest monsoon season it ranges from approximately 2.2 to 3.7 mm per day.

Sunshine duration ranges from about 3.2 to 10.2 hours per day. Maximum sunshine occurs during February, while the months of June to August record the lowest sunshine due to persistent cloud cover. Generally, higher sunshine hours occur between November and May.

Wind speeds in the region range from approximately 2.1 to 3.3 km/h. Higher wind speeds are typically observed from March to June, while lower speeds occur from September to December.

Potential evapotranspiration (PET) values are lower than monthly rainfall during the period from May to October, indicating a water surplus that may contribute to groundwater recharge. Monthly PET values range from approximately 119.3 mm to 177.0 mm.
