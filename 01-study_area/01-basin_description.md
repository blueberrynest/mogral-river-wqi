# Mogral River Basin Description

> *Extracted from: Evaluation of Water Quality Index for River Mogral (2021)*
> *Source pages (PDF): 11, 22, 23*

Mogral Puthur is a gram panchayat located in the Kasaragod district of Kerala, India. The region consists of two villages, Puthur and Kudlu. Mogral Puthur is bounded by the Arabian Sea to the west, Madhur Panchayat to the east, and the Mogral River to the north.

The topography of Mogral Puthur ranges from small hills and slopes to flat plains. The Mogral River forms the northern boundary and a large part of the eastern boundary of the panchayat and supports much of the local agriculture. Several small monsoon-fed rivulets occur throughout the region. Low-lying areas near the river and its tributaries are highly fertile, while the central part of the panchayat gradually gains elevation. Rich laterite deposits are found in the central and eastern areas.

The Mogral River is a west-flowing river located in Kasaragod district. The river derives its name from Mogral, the coastal village situated along its northern bank. It originates in the Kanathur region of the Karadka Reserve Forest and flows for approximately 34 kilometres before discharging into the Arabian Sea near Mogral Puthur. The river basin covers an area of approximately 132 km².

Major tributaries of the Mogral River include Nettipadi and Muliyar streams. After flowing in a north-westerly direction, the river is joined by another stream originating from the northern part of the Karadka Reserve Forest. From Yedhir, the river follows a meandering course through the relatively flat regions of Madhur and Patla before forming a stretch of backwaters near its mouth.

The river basin supports agricultural activities including paddy cultivation and plantation crops. Built-up areas are concentrated mainly along the southern edge of the river, while wasteland occurs along the northern side. The basin also contains several water bodies and paddy fields within the Mogral Puthur and Madhur panchayats.

The soils within the basin vary across terrain types. Gravelly lateritic soils dominate the midland and hilly areas, while alluvial and sandy soils are common in the coastal plains.
