# Potential Sources of Pollution

> *Extracted from: Evaluation of Water Quality Index for River Mogral (2021)*  
> *Source pages (PDF): 11, 12, 13*

Several anthropogenic activities contribute to the degradation of water quality in the Mogral River basin.

## Sand Mining

River sand is widely used for construction because it requires less processing and is often considered superior to other sources of sand. However, excessive sand mining can significantly damage river systems. It alters the natural riverbed, forces the river to change course, and can lead to bank erosion and flooding. Sand extraction also destroys habitats of aquatic organisms and can negatively affect groundwater recharge.

## Agricultural Runoff

Agricultural activities contribute significantly to water pollution. Farms release large quantities of agrochemicals, organic matter, drug residues, sediments, and saline drainage into nearby water bodies. Runoff from agricultural land can carry excess nutrients such as nitrogen and phosphorus into rivers, lakes, and groundwater systems. These nutrients may degrade water quality and disturb aquatic ecosystems.

## Domestic Waste

Domestic waste materials such as paper, cardboard, wood, fruit and vegetable residues, and other household refuse can enter river systems. These wastes may produce both chemical and biological impacts on aquatic environments. During decomposition, organic wastes consume dissolved oxygen in the water, which can harm aquatic plants, fish, and other organisms.

## Bathing and Washing Activities

Bathing and washing activities in rivers introduce soaps and detergents containing chemical compounds that may pollute the water. These substances can degrade water quality and may also harm aquatic life.
