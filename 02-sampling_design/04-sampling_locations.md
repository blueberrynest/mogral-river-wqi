# 4.1 SAMPLING LOCATION

 The Mogral River is a west-flowing river in the Kasaragod district of Kerala. It gets its name
from Mogral, the coastal village on its northern banks. The river empties into the Arabian Sea
in MogralPuthur. It has a length of 34 kilometres and a drainage area of 132 (119.997) square
kilometres.
 
 The river originates in Kanathur in the Karadka Reserve Forest, in Kasaragod. Nettipadi and
Muliyar are the tributaries feeding into the river. After flowing in a north-westerly direction, it
is joined by another stream rising from the northern part of Karadka Reserve Forest. From
Yedhir, the river takes a meandering course and flows through the fairly flat regions of Madhur
and Patla. Toward the end of its course, it forms a long stretch of backwaters several kilometres
long.
 
 The river provide ample irrigation facilities. Mogral is a river of plains and the landuse is
mainly dry land cultivation. The built-up are plenty along the southern edge and waste land
along the northern edge. There are no tributaries and small streams from valleys on either side
join the river. There is no forest inside the river basin. The Mogral Puthur and Madhur
Panchayats have considerable amount of water bodies and paddy fields.
 
 The river flows through paddy fields, plantations etc. The soil is gravelly laterite in midland and
hilly areas where as alluvial and sandy in coastal plains. The annual average rainfall is about
3600 mm. The major source of rainfall is southwest monsoon from June to September which
contributes nearly 85.3% of the total rainfall of the year. The northeast monsoon contributes
nearly 8.9% and a balance of 5.8% is received during the month of January to May as pre
monsoon showers.
